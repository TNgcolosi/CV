My Code Community

I believe that access to quality education can affirm the humanity of all people and I have made it my life’s work to use my experience and insights to create paths and knock down the barriers to a better existence for my fellow citizens. Below are some pictures of projects that I have worked on that have focused on exposing young learners from disadvantaged backgrounds to programming and time on computers in general. I include this because these experiences are what sparked my interest in programming, these are the young people who inspired me to learn what I now know.

While working in the high schools in Mfuleni, it occured to me that helping these students move from 20% to 40% in maths was not the best way to implement real change in their school career, real change that would alter the trajectory of their life beyond school, and so what was the point? I started brainstorming and Google quickly caught me up on industry 4.0 and I realised that the best thing to do for my students would be to find a way to bring tech into their learning spaces or to take them to tech learning spaces.

And so I endeavoured to create a small tech focues program off the side of the maths program where I selected a few students who showed dedication to their studies. At this point I knew nothing about anything to do with technology, I networked at tech events and attended edtech meetups where I left with a dizzy head every time. I managed to find some people who were willing to tutor my students or invite them to learning spaces. This is how I found Code4CT and Coder Dojo which is a learning space at the Cape Town Science Center. It was by seeing what my students were learning at Code4CT that I realised that I want and need to enter this world of code as a coder, and so I became a coder.

I will forever be endebted to m students for inspiring this leap into the deep end of code. Whereever I find the time I continue to tutor, mentor and inspire young people. To date, I have contributed as a code tutor/mentor at: Isiphiwo Primary School in Harare, Coder Dojo, Wordcamp 2018, Tedx Youth, Kasi Gamerz in Gugulethu as well as within the schools that I worked at in Mfuleni.
